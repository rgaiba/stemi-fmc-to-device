**Title.** A National Analysis of the Additional Drive Time to a Second PCI-Capable Hospital

**Background.** Time to reperfusion drives outcomes in ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI). To minimize transport time, emergency medical services default to the nearest percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI)-capable hospital. Because door-to-balloon time varies substantially across U.S. PCI hospitals, the nearest center may not always be the fastest path to reperfusion. The additional drive time to reach a second PCI-capable hospital, and the geographic distribution of that choice, have not been quantified.

**Methods.** We computed the additional drive time between each U.S. census block group's nearest and second-nearest PCI-capable hospital within a 90-mile radius, using OSRM road-network routing from population-weighted centroids. PCI-capable hospitals (n=1,598) were identified from the CMS Provider of Services file and MedPAR 2024 AMI-PCI volume. Estimates were weighted by adults aged 20 years or older from the 2019-2023 American Community Survey; 95% confidence intervals were derived by block-group cluster bootstrap (500 replicates).

**Results.** Across 234,690 block groups representing 245.3 million adults nationally, the population-weighted median additional drive time to a second PCI-capable hospital was 5.1 minutes (95% CI, 5.0-5.1). Approximately 122 million adults (49%) lived within 5 additional minutes of a second PCI-capable hospital, 196 million (79%) within 15 additional minutes, and 226 million (91%) within 30 additional minutes. Applying a STEMI incidence of 1 per 1,000 adults per year, these thresholds corresponded to approximately 122,000, 196,000, and 226,000 STEMI patients per year, respectively. This geographic opportunity for PCI-capable hospital choice was broadly distributed across all four U.S. Census regions, with median regional values of 4.3 minutes in the Northeast, 4.7 minutes in the Midwest, 4.9 minutes in the West, and 6.0 minutes in the South.

**Conclusions.** Nationally, the additional drive time to reach a second PCI-capable hospital is small relative to the between-hospital variation in door-to-balloon time reported across U.S. PCI centers. These findings define the scale and geographic distribution of PCI-capable hospital choice within existing STEMI systems of care. Further work is needed to determine whether incorporating hospital-level performance into destination selection may improve reperfusion timeliness or clinical outcomes.
